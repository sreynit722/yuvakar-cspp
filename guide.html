Study of Urban Development

Studies by WBG focusing on Phnom Penh.

Phnom Penh, the capital of Cambodia, has experienced remarkable growth over the past two decades. Rapid urbanization, population increases, and foreign investment have transformed the city into the nation’s economic and commercial center. The population has grown to more than 2.5 million, driving demand for housing, transportation, and public services. Modern skyscrapers, business districts, and infrastructure projects have reshaped the city’s skyline. However, rapid expansion has also created challenges, including traffic congestion, environmental concerns, and pressure on public utilities. To sustain future growth, Phnom Penh must balance economic development with effective urban planning, environmental protection, and improved quality of life.
